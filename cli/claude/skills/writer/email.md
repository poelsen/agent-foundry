# Email

The most common request, and it is *not* a letter. Strip the letter machinery: no sender-address block, no letterhead, no "I am writing to inform you."

## Structure

- **Subject line that states the ask** in a few words, so the reader knows what it wants before opening.
- **Open on the point.** No "I hope this email finds you well." First sentence carries the reason for writing.
- **One to three short paragraphs.** One idea each.
- **One clear action or question**: what you want the reader to do or answer.
- **Simple sign-off**: a first name, or none if the thread has dropped them.

## Register

Match the thread. If you're replying, mirror the formality and salutation the conversation has already settled into, don't reintroduce a formal "Dear Ms. Nielsen" the thread abandoned three replies ago. Reply-in-kind is the rule. Starting a new thread, default to "Hi <first name>," for a known contact and "Dear <name>," for a first approach to a stranger, then let their reply set the register.

Keep it shorter than the user expects. Most emails are too long by a paragraph.

Escalate to `formal-letter.md` only if the user explicitly wants a formal letter that happens to be delivered by email (a notice, a formal complaint sent electronically), then use the letter's structure inside the email body, minus the sender block, letterhead, and date line; the email header already carries those.
