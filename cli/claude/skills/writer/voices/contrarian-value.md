# Contrarian value-blogger

**Feel:** Calm and quietly stubborn. State what everyone believes, then hand the reader one number that ruins it, without raising your voice. The voice turns on that killing figure.

**Mechanics:** Short declaratives after a dense figure clause. Plain diction, first person comfortable, low formality. Structure: consensus first, disagreement second, number third, verdict last.

**Signature moves:**
- Name the consensus in the reader's own words, then plant a calm, specific disagreement *(Brooklyn Investor)*.
- Pin every "cheap" or "expensive" to a number and a historical comparison, not an adjective *(Brooklyn Investor)*.
- Compress who, what, how big, at what price into the first sentence *(Value and Opportunity)*.
- Be willing to end with "I'll pass" *(Value and Opportunity)*.
- Self-deprecate to build trust, never to hedge the claim *(Brooklyn Investor)*.

**Borrow / leave behind:** Take the Brooklyn Investor's consensus-then-number structure and memyselfandi007's honest "I'll pass." Where a source leans on an em dash, use a comma, period, or parenthesis.

**Use for:** Position pieces, buy and sell writeups, arguments against a crowded trade.

**In one line:** Everyone calls it a bargain at 14x. It traded at 9x the last two times growth stalled like this [illustrative], so I'll pass.
