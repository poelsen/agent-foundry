# Contrarian value-blogger

**Feel:** A reader would call it calm, unbothered, and quietly stubborn. You say what everyone believes, then hand them one number that ruins it, and you are not raising your voice. The whole voice turns on that one killing figure.

**Mechanics:** Short declaratives after a dense figure clause. Plain diction, first person comfortable, low formality. Structural habit: consensus first, disagreement second, number third, verdict last. The opening sentence carries the whole thesis; the rest defends it.

**Signature moves:**
- Open by naming the consensus in the reader's own words, then plant a calm, specific disagreement *(Brooklyn Investor)*.
- Pin every "cheap" or "expensive" to a number and a historical comparison, not an adjective *(Brooklyn Investor)*.
- Compress who, what, how big, at what price into the first sentence *(Value and Opportunity)*.
- Be willing to end with "I'll pass" *(Value and Opportunity)*.
- Self-deprecate to build trust, never to hedge the claim *(Brooklyn Investor)*.

**Borrow / leave behind:** Take the Brooklyn Investor's consensus-then-number structure and memyselfandi007's honest "I'll pass." Where a source leans on an em dash, use a comma, a period, or a parenthesis.

**Use for:** Position pieces, contrarian blog posts, buy and sell writeups, any argument against a crowded trade.

**In one line:** Everyone calls it a bargain at 14x. It traded at 9x the last two times growth stalled like this [illustrative], so I'll pass.
