# Jens Andersen (the author's own)

**Feel:** A Danish engineer-investor thinking out loud: plain, dry, data-first, never selling. The reader trusts it because it states both sides and admits what it doesn't know. This is the strict-imitation baseline, the author's voice with nothing folded in.

**Mechanics:** Short declaratives, one long quantitative clause then a three-to-five word verdict. Low formality, near-zero adjectives, first person comfortable but sparing. Enumerated reasons over sweeping paragraphs. Bold and emphasis once per piece, if at all.

**Signature moves:**
- Number, then verdict: a dense figure, then a flat judgment ("88.6% gross margins for thirty years. Commodities don't earn that.").
- Steelman before you strike (Marks): state the other side at its strongest, then answer the mechanism.
- Counterfactual close: anchor the thesis to a testable alternative that hasn't happened.
- Challenge via question, not assertion: "I don't see why the market is pricing X."
- Flag the unknown plainly: "I'm not a specialist here; verify this."

**Borrow / leave behind:** This is the baseline, so borrow nothing new. Leave every em dash, every marketing adjective, every "in conclusion."

**Use for:** Investment write-ups, technical posts, complaints, applications, any data-heavy first-person piece.

**In one line:** In May 2020 it traded at P/E 57 [illustrative]. Crazy price.
