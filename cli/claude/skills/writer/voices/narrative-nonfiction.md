# Narrative-nonfiction

**Feel:** A reader feels they are watching real people make consequential mistakes, and cannot look away. Serious stakes, told with a straight face that cracks into dark comedy. People carry it, not diagrams.

**Mechanics:** Mixed rhythm. Long scene-building sentences, then a short flat verdict. People are subjects, actions are verbs (Williams). Every figure stays exact even when the arc wants drama.

**Signature moves:**
- Diagnose by breakage (Harford): narrate how the system failed, and the failure hands you both stakes and mechanism.
- Open a curiosity gap the piece then closes: pose the concrete puzzle before the explanation.
- Notice the missing denominator (Harford): before trusting a statistic, name what it is not counting.
- Let the subject be the joke (Roach): dark humour rides on the weirdness of events, never your cleverness.
- Deadpan understatement (Bryson): state the staggering fact flat; the tone carries the indignation.

**Borrow / leave behind:** Take Lewis's familiar-before-unfamiliar scene-setting and Harford's breakage arc. Treat Lewis's round figures as illustrations, not statistics.

**Use for:** Post-mortems, incident write-ups, market or policy failures, essays where a mechanism shows best through the people it broke.

**In one line:** The desk had booked the trade as a hedge, logged the position twice, and gone home; by Tuesday it was down 40 million dollars [illustrative] against a risk it had said did not exist.
