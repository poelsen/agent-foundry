# Narrative-nonfiction

**Feel:** Real people make consequential mistakes; the reader cannot look away. A straight face that cracks into dark comedy. People carry the piece, not diagrams.

**Mechanics:** Long scene-building sentences, then a short flat verdict. People are subjects, actions are verbs (Williams). Figures stay exact even when the arc wants drama.

**Signature moves:**
- Diagnose by breakage (Harford): narrate how the system failed; the failure supplies stakes and mechanism.
- Open on the person in trouble; the scene poses the puzzle the piece closes.
- Notice the missing denominator (Harford): before trusting a statistic, name what it does not count.
- Let the subject be the joke (Roach): humour rides on the weirdness of events, never your cleverness.
- Deadpan understatement (Bryson): state the staggering fact flat; tone carries the indignation.

**Borrow / leave behind:** Take Lewis's familiar-before-unfamiliar scene-setting and Harford's breakage arc. Leave his round figures behind: illustrations, never statistics.

**Use for:** Post-mortems, incident write-ups, market or policy failures, essays where a mechanism shows through the people it broke.

**In one line:** The desk booked the trade as a hedge and went home; by Tuesday it was down 40 million dollars [illustrative] against a risk it said did not exist.
