# The house blend (recommended default)

**Feel:** Unmistakably the author: plain, dry, data-first, honest about what he doesn't know. Reads a little easier and lands a little harder than the baseline, without the reader noticing why.

**Mechanics:** Short-to-medium sentences, number-then-verdict as the spine, first person only to mark judgment. Same diction and hard-negatives as the baseline; borrowed moves add clarity, never new tics.

**Signature moves:**
- Hold the author's centre: bind each number to one sentence of story *(Damodaran)*, steelman then strike, close respecting the reader's judgment.
- Add at most one or two per piece: an everyday analogy before a dense metric *(Jackson)*; a letter to one named non-expert, unknowables disclaimed openly *(Buffett; Marks)*; one big number anchored to something physical *(Bryson)*; one rationed dry concept-handle *(Alexander)*.

**Borrow / leave behind:** Take the move, never the source's surface tics: Bryson's physical anchor without the awe, the concept-handle without the coinage-chasing. Dominance rule: when a borrowed move and the baseline collide, Jens's voice and the hard-negatives win.

**Use for:** The default for the author's own public and analytical writing: blog posts, investment theses, data-heavy pieces.

**In one line:** A turnstile clicking once a second, all year, is 31.5M fares [illustrative]. The agency still can't fund its maintenance backlog.
