# Formal / business letter

Register: formal, courteous, direct on the ask. One purpose per letter. One page.

## Structure

- **Sender block, recipient block, and date** at the top; date in the locale's format (`6 July 2026` for GB/EU).
- **Salutation and sign-off must match**: never mix registers:
  - British, recipient unknown: **Dear Sir or Madam,** → **Yours faithfully,** (comma after both, never a colon; open punctuation, no comma on either, is also correct if applied to both).
  - British, recipient named: **Dear Ms Nielsen,** → **Yours sincerely,** (no full stop after Ms in British style).
  - US: **Dear Mr./Ms. Name:** (colon) → **Sincerely,**.
- **One purpose**, stated early and unambiguously. What you want, and by when if relevant.
- Hold register steady throughout, one pronoun, one tense (Zinsser). A formal letter that drifts chatty undercuts its own authority.

## Politeness calibration

Danish and Nordic directness can read as brusque to a British or American recipient. Soften the ask with a light relational frame, *without* flattery or filler. "I would be grateful if you could confirm this by Friday" carries the same demand as "Confirm this by Friday" but travels better across the North Sea. Keep the ask itself unambiguous; soften only the framing, never the substance.
